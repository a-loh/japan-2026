Disney Days — Good to Know

  • 💧 Bring water bottles — water and sealed non-alcoholic drinks (PET bottles, thermos) are allowed in both parks. Pack refillable bottles for the July heat. (No cans, glass, or alcohol.)
  • 🛂 Security at every entry — bag check + metal detector on entry and re-entry. Leave any pocket knife/multitool behind; keep meds in their labeled packaging.
  • 🍱 Outside food — generally not allowed, but Candice's allergy lets her bring safe snacks (see guide below). Otherwise eat in-park, or step out and re-enter.
  • 🎟️ Tickets — date-specific and non-refundable, but the date is changeable online if plans shift. Some ticket types use facial recognition at entry.
  • 👕 Dress — casual; no adult full-body costumes or face-covering masks. Selfie-sticks aren't allowed (hand grips are fine).

Candice's Vegetarian + Allergy Guide

  • Konbini protein staples (available 24/7 everywhere): atsuage (18g/100g), natto (17g/pack), edamame (11g/cup), hard tofu, soy milk, hard-boiled eggs
  • Orange allergy — avoid yuzu (柚子) + ponzu (ポン酢). Yuzu is a mandarin orange hybrid used widely in Japanese cooking. Always ask before eating sauces, dressings, and ramen broths. Request soy sauce (醤油) instead of ponzu.
  • Allergy card (print + show at restaurants):
    私はベジタリアンです。肉、魚、出汁を含まないお料理をお願いします。
    オレンジと柚子のアレルギーがあります。柚子、ポン酢、柑橘類を避けてください。
    I am vegetarian — no meat, fish, or dashi. I am allergic to oranges and yuzu. No yuzu, ponzu, or citrus please.
  • No shared cooking surfaces — won't eat food grilled on the same surface as meat. Teppanyaki (chef-controlled, separate area) and kushikatsu (individual frying) are OK.
  • Best meal of the trip: Shojin ryori at Shigetsu, Kyoto (100% vegan, 40g+ protein, zen garden view, no citrus in traditional Buddhist cuisine)
  • Disney parks: Look for the green leaf icon on menus — official plant-based items at 10+ restaurants
  • Backup protein: Pack 1-2 boxes of vegan protein powder + bars from Canada for packed theme park days
